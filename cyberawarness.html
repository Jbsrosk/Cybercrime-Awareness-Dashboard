    Legal framework

    Cybercrime Act 2025

    Passed by Liberian Senate • In force 2025

    Liberia's landmark legislation criminalizes digital fraud, unauthorized system access, identity theft, online harassment, and data breaches. It provides the legal foundation for Liberia's growing digital economy and aligns with international cybercrime frameworks. Businesses are now legally required to protect customer data and may face liability for breaches resulting from negligence.

    Covers: fraud, identity theft, data breaches, harassment, unauthorized access

    National Cybersecurity Strategy 2025–2029

    Liberia Telecommunications Authority • Launched February 2026

    An 8-priority roadmap covering universal connectivity, digital inclusion, a national digital addressing system, cybersecurity and data sovereignty, e-government, and ICT-driven innovation. Built around four strategic pillars designed to deliver measurable outcomes and reduce the digital divide in rural areas.

    Pillars: connectivity, inclusion, security, e-governance

    LCCPMA — Liberia Cyber Crime Prevention & Mitigation Agency

    Ministry of Posts & Telecommunications

    The national body mandated to provide cybersecurity education, digital forensics training, technical assistance to law enforcement, and cyber defensive capabilities to business communities. The LCCPMA also deploys free cybersecurity toolkits to government ministries and works with journalists, businesses, and the public to build a culture of cyber awareness.

    Free resources: toolkits, training, incident support

    ECOWAS Regional Cybersecurity Cooperation

    West Africa Regional Framework

    Liberia is a member of the ECOWAS cybersecurity framework, which enables cross-border intelligence sharing, harmonized standards, and capacity-building across all 15 West African member states. Through ECOWAS alignment, Liberia can access international technical assistance and funding for cybersecurity capacity building.

    Portal: cyberportal.ecowas.int
